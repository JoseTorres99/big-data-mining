Manilyn Reynes-Manaloto[a] (born Manilyn Villamor Reynes on April 27, 1972), is a Filipina actress, singer and television presenter.[2] She is regarded as a Philippine pop culture icon and dubbed as the Star of All Decades.[3][4] She has achieved mainstream success across film, music and television. She is currently an exclusive talent of GMA Network.[5][6][7] She has received accolades from the FAMAS Awards, Awit Awards, and Metro Manila Film Festival.[8][9] She is the fourth actress with the most wins (3) at the PMPC Star Awards for TV in the "Best Comedy Actress" category.

Early life and education

[edit]

Reynes was born on April 27, 1972, in Manila and grew up in Cebu. Her parents are Nelson Clarin Reynes and Lounching Reynes.[10] Her family moved to Cebu when she was only a few days old.[11] She studied in Terejo Elementary School until third grade.[11] She attended University of Southern Philippines Foundation (USPF) in Cebu during her fourth and mid-fifth grade before leaving for Manila.[11][12]

Career

[edit]

Prior to acting, Reynes started her career by joining a singing contest in Cebu. She was only 8 years old when she became a 7 weeks Champion in Weewee Jamboree Singing contest, and came in second place at the grand finals.[12]

After her family moved back to Manila, she was launched as a child actor in Regal Films, To Mama with Love (1983).[13] She also appeared in the movie Minsan, May Isang Ina (1983) for which she won the FAMAS Award for Best Child Actress in 1984.[14][15] She also became a recording artist under Ivory Records and released three albums.[12]

As a teenager, Reynes was one of the original members of the television variety show That's Entertainment (1986).[11] In 1988, she signed with OctoArts International and released a self-titled album, Manilyn, featuring her hit single Sayang na Sayang which became a commercial success and earned her a platinum award.[11][12] She continued to release 10 more albums under OctoArts International (known as PolyEast Records). Reynes had a concert in Araneta Coliseum and Ultra Stadium in 1990. She was also the youngest artist to hold a concert at the Araneta Coliseum in that same year.[11]

Personal life

[edit]

Reynes previously dated actor Janno Gibbs.They were a popular loveteam in the 80s until their break up in 1989.[16]

She met her husband, Aljon Jimenez in the variety show, That's Entertainment but did not take notice of each other until they worked together in the movie, Shake, Rattle & Roll II in 1990.[17] Reynes and Jimenez was in a situationship, acting like a real-life couple but without commitment. Then Jimenez disappeared and cut off all their communication. In 1991, he surprised Reynes on her 19th birthday after a year-long without contact.[17] The two had an argument until she found out that he disappeared out of jealousy.[17]

Reynes married former matinee idol and actor Aljon Jimenez in Reno, Nevada U.S.A. in 1996. They have three children, Kyle, Kirk and Kael Jimenez[18]
